# Gambling and games

> [!NOTE]
> To ensure consistency worldwide, Microsoft Advertising enforces our policies across our ad network using the English version. We provide translated versions as a courtesy to our clients, though the translations are not meant to change the content or meaning of the official policy language.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This policy applies to all ad types.

> [!NOTE]
> This page was last updated on May 13, 2026.

Microsoft may allow advertising, with market restrictions, for websites that promote or facilitate gambling. Gambling ads must always comply with the target markets. Gambling can be defined as one or more of the following:

- Any content that facilitates, promotes, or is affiliated in any way with online gambling or wagering of any type.
- Sites that accept wagers or require payment in exchange for the chance to win cash prizes or prizes with monetary value, as well as sites that offer both information and links related primarily to the promotion of online gambling.
- Games of chance or skill that involve real money.
- Sports betting
- Sportsbooks
- Daily fantasy sports
- Spread betting
- Lotteries
- In-person casinos (brick-and-mortar facilities) in markets where legal restrictions exist
- Educational, “learn to play,” “practice,” and other free simulation sites, including .net sites that are affiliated with an online gambling site.

## Gambling certification

Advertisers must apply to participate in the Gambling and Contests Advertising Program and agree to the Terms & Conditions. If you would like to apply, please complete, and submit the [Gambling and Contests Advertising Program Participation Form](https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=2262174).

## Requirements for All Gambling Advertisers

- Advertisers must comply with all applicable regulations including licensing and/or registration requirements in each market where their ads are serving. Not all markets allow gambling advertising. **Please refer to the list of preapproved gambling markets listed in the market quick reference section below.** Proof of eligibility/license is required.
- Geo-targeting and age targeting for 18+, or the market legal gambling age, whichever is higher, are required. Specific market targeting policies are listed in the market restrictions section below.
- Advertising designed to appeal to users under the market's minimum targeting age is not allowed. Advertising must not be served in a context where it is highly likely it will be displayed to users under the legal age.

  - This includes, but it is not limited to, the use of language, themes, expressions, graphic resources, audio or visual elements that may appeal to underage individuals or using individuals who are or appear to be underage in promotional content or on the website.
- Ads must only target approved countries. Sites must not accept or procure bets from residents of markets where online gambling is disallowed.
- Ads that are acceptable under one country's policy guidelines might not be acceptable under another. Each country's requirements must be followed.
- Advertisers must comply with the target market's responsible gaming requirements.

## Responsible gaming messaging

- Advertising must not misrepresent or glorify the benefits of gambling or encourage people to play beyond their means.
- Advertising may not imply or suggest that gambling is a viable alternative to employment or financial investments, a way to recover from financial losses, or that skills may affect the outcome of a game of chances.
- Information on the odds of winning and prize amounts must be described accurately and must not be misrepresented. Advertisers may not present winning as the most probable outcome of the game, nor misrepresent a person's chance of winning a prize.

**Example:**

| Allowed | Not allowed |
| --- | --- |
| **Enter for a chance to win $10,000** | Enter to win $10,000 |

## Games of Skill

Games that offer cash payouts where the outcome is predominantly determined by the player’s level of skill are generally allowed. Skill-based games include (but are not limited to):

- Chess
- Puzzle games
- Daily Fantasy sports (in most jurisdictions; see details by market above).
- Free cell solitaire

Advertisers must apply to participate in the Gambling and Contests Advertising Program and agree to the Terms & Conditions. If you would like to apply, please complete, and submit the [Gambling and Contests Advertising Program Participation Form](https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=2262174).

## Games of Chance or Luck

Advertisers that offer cash payouts for games that are predominantly determined by chance or luck are considered gambling and must follow the market-specific gambling policies above. Chance-based games include (but are not limited to):

- Bingo
- Poker
- Slots
- Roulette
- Scratch cards
- Klondike Draw Solitaire
- Blackjack

Advertisers must apply to participate in the Gambling and Contests Advertising Program and agree to the Terms & Conditions. If you would like to apply, please complete, and submit the [Gambling and Contests Advertising Program Participation Form](https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=2262174).

## Social or Sweepstakes Gaming

Social gaming sites simulate gambling but are played with virtual credits. There is no real-money wagering and no opportunity to win anything of monetary value.

Advertisers that offer cash payouts or prizes that can be exchanged for cash are considered gambling under our policies and must follow the market-specific gambling policies above.

Social gaming advertisers must:

- Be clear that no real money or prizes of monetary value are involved.
- Not offer cash or cash-value payouts as prizes. This includes:

  - Cash or cash equivalents
  - Virtual currency
  - Credits redeemable for cash or prizes
  - Prizes that can be sold, traded, or exchanged for money. Including but not limited to merchandise, tickets, or in-game items.
- Hold a valid license for distribution in markets that require licenses.
- Target users according to that targeted market’s age requirements.
- Not use brand names or logos from real-money casinos or lotteries.
- Not have links to online casino or sports betting sites.

Aggregator and affiliate advertisers are prohibited.

Advertisers must apply to participate in the Gambling and Contests Advertising Program and agree to the Terms & Conditions. If you would like to apply, please complete, and submit the [Gambling and Contests Advertising Program Participation Form](https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=2262174).

## Sweepstakes, contests, and competitions

Advertising that promotes participation based on payment or consideration is subject to the same restrictions listed above for permitted gambling sites in markets that allow gambling. Such programs may include, but are not limited to, random drawings, spin-to-win games, trivia contests, word games, and essay contests.

Advertising that promotes any contest with a specific offer, or where participation is subject to specific terms, must include qualifying text that is relevant to the offer in the ad and must indicate in the ad that additional terms and conditions apply.

If participation is contingent on another action, such as signing up for a service, or if specific terms apply, these prerequisites or terms should be set forth clearly and prominently on the website before the site visitor signs up.
