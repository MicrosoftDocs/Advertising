# Lodging property categories

Microsoft Advertising categorizes lodging accommodations as [hotels](#hotels) or [vacation rentals](#vacation-rentals)—listed in your property feed as "hotels" and "vacation_rentals," respectively. The lodging category you choose determines the searches in which your property appears. By choosing the correct category, you can better help customers find your property. Learn more about [property feeds details](https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=2255125).

To help potential customers make the right choice, we showcase a badge onto your ad, which designates whether the property offering is a hotel or vacation rental.

Each category contains different property types. A hotel property typically has a front desk or on-site management, accepts overnight guests, and publicly lists its business address. If your property is lacking any of these, then it may be a vacation rental. If you're still wondering how we categorize your property, please see the non-exhaustive list below.

> [!NOTE]
> The name of your business doesn't change its category. For instance, a property named Contoso Hotel may indeed be classified as a bed and breakfast, rather than a hotel.

<a id="hotels"></a>

## Hotels

Properties in the hotel category should be able to accept overnight guests and must have a front desk, manager, and/or owner on site. Hotel properties should also have a publicly listed business address and property name.

| Hotel property type | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Bed and breakfast (sometimes known as a guest house or pension)** | A bed and breakfast is a private or small commercial property that usually has:<br>- A shared kitchen or provides home-cooked meals<br>- The owner or manager present |
| **Budget Japanese inn (also known as Minshuku)** | A budget Japanese inn is a traditional inn where guests sleep in Japanese-style accommodations. It may:<br>- Be family-run<br>- Have tatami mat flooring<br>- Offer a futon for sleeping |
| **Camping cabin** | A camping cabin typically contains cabins or cottages and offers:<br>- Options for travelers to camp in a tent or bring their recreational vehicle<br>- Plumbing or climate control that may or may not be on site<br>- Shared facilities, such as kitchens, bathrooms, and other amenities |
| **Capsule hotel or pod hotel** | A capsule hotel or a pod hotel usually offers:<br>- Compact sleeping spaces arranged in rows and often stacked<br>- Shared amenities, such as lockers and bathrooms |
| **Extended stay hotel or aparthotel** | An extended stay hotel or aparthotel is a self-catered, fully furnished apartment containing:<br>- A front desk with limited operating hours<br>- An in-unit kitchen |
| **Farm stay** | A farm stay is a working farm, ranch, or vineyard that:<br>- Has on-site management<br>- Has workers or farmhands present<br>- Provides overnight stays |
| **Hostel** | A hostel offers affordable, dorm-style (bunk bed) rooms and can:<br>- Cater to travelers within a common age group who may share similar interests<br>- Offer private rooms for families or groups<br>- Require a membership |
| **Hotel** | A hotel provides basic services like room service and professional housekeeping, and typically:<br>- Has a front desk (even with limited operating hours) |
| **Inn** | An inn is primarily a brewery, cafe, pub, or restaurant that:<br>- Also offers lodging<br>- Uses fewer than 20 rooms for lodging |
| **Japanese inn or Ryokan** | A Japanese inn (sometimes known as a Ryokan) is a traditional inn where guests sleep in a Japanese-style room. It may have:<br>- A bed or a futon for sleeping<br>- Amenities that range from basic to luxurious<br>- Locations in both rural and urban areas |
| **Love hotel** | A love hotel is for adults only and is:<br>- Oriented around privacy for romantic activities |
| **Motel** | A motel is a roadside hotel geared towards motorists, which usually has:<br>- Parking directly outside the guest room<br>- Rooms arranged in low buildings |
| **Mountain hut or lodge** | A mountain hut or lodge is located in a rural area often by a forest, lake, mountain, or river. It typically has:<br>- A front desk or on-site host<br>- A single building or several cottages<br>- Nature-related activities, such as canoeing, fishing, hiking, kayaking, or skiing |
| **Religious accommodation** | A religious accommodation is lodging used for religious purposes, and is:<br>- Associated with a religion, but doesn’t require guests to practice any religion<br>- Located within a religious property, such as a church, dharmasala, monastery, or synagogue |
| **Resort hotel** | A resort hotel is usually spread out over more land than a hotel and offers:<br>- Athletic or social activities<br>- Extra amenities, such as beach access, skiing, and spa treatments<br>- Restaurants or all-inclusive dining options |

## Vacation rentals

Vacation rental properties must provide a furnished space that's private for the guest who's renting it and cleaned in between stays. A vacation rental must also be managed and accept short-term reservations.

| Vacation rental property type | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Apartment or flat** | An apartment or flat:<br>- Has other residents living within the area<br>- Is located in a multi-unit building or complex |
| **Bungalow** | A bungalow is a single-level home that usually has:<br>- A front porch<br>- A sloping roof |
| **Cabin** | A cabin is a small house, which is often:<br>- Built with logs or wood<br>- Located by the forest or mountains |
| **Chalet** | A chalet is a house built with wood and has sloping roofs. It's usually:<br>- Found in popular ski locations<br>- Used as a summer home |
| **Cottage** | A cottage is a cozy house that's typically:<br>- Located in rural areas near a beach or lake |
| **Gite** | A gite is a vacation home found in French-speaking regions of Europe, which is often:<br>- A converted cottage or barn<br>- Close to a primary residence<br>- Found in rural, countryside locations |
| **Holiday village rental** | A holiday village rental is a unit in a self-catered resort where visitors:<br>- Have access to a central area with amenities, entertainment, and shops<br>- Stay in apartments or villas co-located with other residences |
| **House** | A house is a stand-alone residential building that may share:<br>- A wall (like a duplex)<br>- An outdoor space |
| **Villa** | A villa is a luxurious house that usually has:<br>- A swimming pool<br>- Indoor and outdoor spaces<br>- Large gardens |
| **Vacation rental (other)** | Vacation rental (other) is an option if:<br>- The property is a lodging business, but doesn't fall under any of the categories mentioned |
