# Traffic quality center

## Overview

Microsoft is committed to delivering world-class service to advertisers and publisher partners. We’re dedicated to protecting our marketplace from click fraud, conversion fraud, phishing, malware, and maintaining traffic quality standards. Use this resource for information about our traffic quality protection systems and [Policies](hlp_BA_CONC_Policy_Introduction.md).

> [!NOTE]
> Despite our best efforts, we can’t guarantee every instance of low quality, fraudulent activity or suspicious traffic will be caught. If you suspect click or conversion fraud, [Report It to our Team](https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=398371) and we will investigate it promptly.

## Terminology

- **Microsoft Advertising Network Traffic**

  Network Traffic is the term for the ad impressions, clicks, and conversions that show up on Microsoft Bing, AOL, and Yahoo websites or the websites of their search partners that they distribute.

- **Click Quality**

  Microsoft Advertising uses real-time and post-click systems to help guard against low quality and invalid clicks. Advertisers will never be charged for clicks determined by our system to be invalid or low quality.

  Clicks are categorized as standard quality, low quality, and invalid.

  - **Standard quality**: Normal customer ad clicks.
  - **Low quality**: Clicks that exhibit characteristics of unclear commercial intent, and patterns of unusual activity. This includes invalid clicks.
  - **Invalid**: Clicks that exhibit characteristics of user error, search engine robots or fraudulent activity.
  - Once identified as invalid, low-quality clicks won’t affect other performance metrics. Read more about how Microsoft monitors click quality here: [Microsoft Advertising Click Measurement: Description of Methodology](hlp_BA_CONC_Policy_ClickMeasurement.md)

## How Microsoft Advertising Works to Detect and Respond to Invalid Clicks

Invalid clicks are a serious issue for advertisers. We systematically analyze clicks to determine their validity, and, when possible, remove invalid clicks before they are reported to you (removed clicks will never be charged to the advertiser).

If potentially invalid clicks are detected, members of our team will flag the case, examine the affected account(s), and issue refunds where appropriate. See the Network protection page for more details.

We have several methods of identifying suspected invalid clicks and continually work to refine our technology to identify invalid click activity.

## Our Team

- **Traffic quality investigators**: The Microsoft Advertising Traffic Quality team is a customer-focused support team that partners with support agents, proactive engineers, and other Microsoft teams to analyze advertiser complaints involving invalid clicks, fake leads, and malware.

  **How Microsoft Advertising Protects You**

  Microsoft Advertising takes user, advertiser, and network protection seriously. Between Microsoft Advertising and the Microsoft ecosystem of security and privacy products, we ensure that the Microsoft Advertising Search Network provides quality traffic and a safe experience for our customers.

  Here are some specific methods we use to help protect advertisers:

  - **Risk Assessment:** In some markets, our systems evaluate the quality of partner traffic in real- time and may adjust the bids via auto bidding strategies to mitigate risk on advertiser ROI for associated clicks. If this is available in your market, there’s nothing you need to do to take advantage of this. Our models apply automatically based on data available to Microsoft Advertising auto bidding technology.
  - **Removal of Offenders:** When we identify low quality domains from our proactive monitoring or reactive investigations, we reserve the right to remove publishers and/or their traffic. We also employ standard IP block-listing methodologies.
  - **Advertiser Credits:** When we identify invalid activity through our automated systems and reactive investigations, we issue credits to affected advertisers. We also may use those findings to update our automated click filtration system to proactively detect and filter similar activity in the future.
  - **Proactive:** The Microsoft Advertising Platform contains a robust invalid click filtration system, which deploys various algorithms to detect and neutralize invalid clicks. Additionally, Microsoft Advertising engineers proactively review system alerts and data trends to identify suspicious activity, addressing clicks that may have escaped the automated filtration systems and crediting advertiser accounts for those clicks.
  - **Reactive:** Microsoft Advertising support teams work closely with advertisers to address issues with traffic quality. Microsoft Advertising Traffic Quality teams review advertiser complaints around suspicious click activity and work across teams to verify data accuracy and integrity. If an advertiser complaint identifies invalid click activity that escaped the automated filtration system, the Traffic Quality and support teams will process a credit to the advertiser’s account and partner with internal teams to update automated systems for improved detection.

  **Best Practices**

  - **Relevant and Quality Content**

    High-quality original content provides a positive user experience that meets the needs of intended audiences.

  - **Partner Properties Must:**

    - Prominently display accurate contact information (at minimum, name, address, and email).
    - Use a homepage that contains a prominent link to the site's privacy policy.
    - Use current and active SSL certification for all websites. All software must be digitally signed by its author(s) using a valid certificate issued by a reputable certification authority.
    - Comply with all guidelines and applicable laws and regulations in the jurisdiction(s) where they operate.

  - **Disallowed Content**

    Sites containing disallowed content or promoting or enabling any of the following (non-exhaustive) will be rejected, blocked, or taken down:

    - Adult content
    - Alcohol products or promotion
    - Animal cruelty
    - Banned substances
    - Bulk marketing spam products
    - Copyright and intellectual property violations
    - Fireworks and explosives
    - Gambling
    - Exploiting hardship or vulnerable audiences
    - Harmful, dangerous, or violent content including content that threatens public or personal safety, physical, mental, or financial health.
    - Content and targeting intended to degrade, intimidate, or incite violence or prejudicial action against people based on their race, gender, ethnicity, national origin, religion, sexual orientation, disability, or other differentiating factors.
    - Illegal content including content, products, and services of questionable legality.
    - Misleading or harmful content including misrepresentation, unsubstantiated claims, sensationalized text, or images, deceptive or fraudulent practices or content whose primary purpose is to create controversy.
    - Online pharmacies, sites promoting prescription-only medicines or sale of unapproved pharmaceuticals and supplements.
    - Illegal and recreational drugs
    - Sensitive content including tragedy (disaster, war, conflict, crime), controversial or extreme religious and political content.
    - Substantially unmoderated user-generated content
    - Tobacco products, promotion, paraphernalia, and e-cigarettes
    - Weapons of violence and firearms

  - **Proper Implementation and Website Behavior**

    To prevent invalid or low-quality clicks, ads must be implemented properly on approved websites with clear navigation and user controls.

  - **Disallowed Behavior**

    The following behavior is disallowed for partners and websites (non-exhaustive):

    - Use of Microsoft services for illegal or unauthorized purposes
    - Use of invalid or artificial traffic methods including practices that improperly entice, suggest, encourage, or artificially induce users to click
    - Use of malware or unwanted software. Compliance with Microsoft Defender Security requirements and adherence to prevalent industry guidelines and best practices is required.
    - Use of deception, social engineering, or phishing practices
    - Any practice that inhibits Microsoft from gathering, analyzing, or detecting non-compliant content or practices
    - Interference with user navigation, user-initiated actions, or creation of unwanted behavior including hijacking or forced redirects
    - Scaring or manipulating a customer into believing their devices need repair
    - Manipulation of ad delivery and display
    - Delivering ads to pages with entirely or largely imported content.
    - Delivering ads to under-construction or unfinished sites
    - Deliberately mismatching Microsoft Advertiser supported ad language to incorrect locations

    If you see any of these issues, please notify [Microsoft Advertising Support](https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=398371). To access additional information about Microsoft Advertising Publisher policies, refer to the Microsoft PubCenter Policy page guidelines.

  - **How Can I Find the Automated Bid Strategies That are Right for Me?**

    Automated bidding adjusts bids to meet performance goals creating efficient management of complex and time-consuming bids. Automated bidding dynamically adjusts bids at auction time based on set targets and is compatible with various ad types like text ads, Dynamic Search ads, and Shopping campaigns. It also includes strategies like Enhanced CPC for better efficiency, Maximize Clicks for more clicks, Maximize Conversions for more conversions, target CPA for conversions within a target cost per acquisition, and target ROAS for achieving target return on ad spend. Automated bidding is a core component for organizing ad accounts and can help drive campaigns towards performance goals. Let’s look at a few of these examples more closely:

    - **Enhanced CPC (eCPC)** optimizes bids at auction time to maximize performance toward business goals, using real-time auction signals and flexibility to select tailored strategies for any performance goal. Enhanced CPC adjusts your bids in real-time to increase your chances for a conversion.
    - **Target CPA (Cost per Acquisition)** for conversions sets bids to achieve the desired CPA, leveraging real-time data and auction signals for bid optimization, and maintaining budget control while the platform adjusts keyword bids in real time to meet CPA goals.
    - **Target ROAS (Return on ad spend)** automatically sets bids at auction time to help get more revenue or conversion value for the ROAS target set by the advertiser. Benefits include reduced time spent on campaign optimization, increased efficiency of campaigns, and the ability to achieve ROAS targets at scale.
    - **Maximize clicks** automatically sets bids in real time to get as many clicks as possible within your budget. This strategy is available for Search ads and Microsoft Shopping campaigns.
    - **Cost per completed view (CPCV)** is a Premium Streaming campaign ad strategy where you set the amount you want to pay per completed view of Video ads, and Microsoft Advertising optimizes bids to show ads to potential customers likely to view the ad to completion.
    - **Cost per thousand impressions (CPM)** are great for building brand awareness. You set the amount you want to pay per 1,000 view impressions and Microsoft Advertising optimizes your bids to meet your performance goals, whether it's for display ads or online Video ads. (For Video ads, a viewed impression is counted when more than 50% of the video screen is in view for at least two seconds.)

  - **Monitor Ad Click-Through and Conversion Rates**

    Use reports to monitor key trends in your ad’s performance. Understanding your average daily, weekly, and monthly click volume helps you spot sudden, significant changes in click volume, which might be an indication of invalid clicks.

    Some key performance statistics to monitor include:

    - Clicks: The number of times an ad was clicked
    - Click-through rate: The number of times an ad was clicked, divided by the number of impressions.
    - Conversion rate: The number of conversions, divided by the total number of clicks.

    Sudden increases in click volume can be the result of many factors and can often be good for your business. Before you become concerned about an increase in click volume indicating possible fraud, consider if the following might be the cause:

    - Seasonal changes, such as an increase in online shopping during the holidays.
    - Sales, discounts, or promotions that increase visibility and bring extra traffic to your site.
    - Recent news events that stimulate online consumer interest and web traffic.
    - Changes to your ad headings or text, or changes to your website.
    - New links to your website from other sites.

  - **My Website Has Malware — What Happened?**

    You may notice your website has ads, popups, or other content you did not create. Your website could also be serving malware unknowingly and without any obvious signs. There are several ways this can happen:

    - Security vulnerabilities in content management systems and their related plugins
    - Installing templates, plugins, and other types of code from an untrusted source
    - Hacked/phished credentials which are used to infect your website.

    These are just a few examples of how your site may have been compromised. The internet and technology are ever-changing, and the ways in which a site can be infected change along with them.

  - **Identifying and Avoiding Malware**

    A key to avoiding malware is being able to identify common sources and deceptive behaviors. It is not always obvious. Review the following examples of common malware practices. It could save you potential headaches later.

    - **Downloads**: When visiting an unfamiliar webpage that offers a download, be sure to check the source of the software. If there is any doubt of the legitimacy or need of the software offered, research online to determine the reputation and history of the site. Pay special attention to downloads that don’t appear to belong to the site you are visiting. For example, if you have Lightspark flash player installed and you see a prompt from “xyz-website.com” to download an update, take special note that the website asking you to download the file is not “adobe.com,” the software author’s website. Also, be aware of similar looking or misspelled domain names claiming to be the authoritative source when, if you look closely, you’ll see they are not related.
    - **Emails**: Be wary of any incoming communication that has attachments, even if it appears to be from a person or business you know. Antivirus and antimalware software such as Windows Defender is useful for scanning any incoming attachments. Be especially cautious of clicking on any links contained in messages, and always take note of the actual URL that link takes you to before entering any sensitive information such as login details or payment information.
    - **SMS Phishing**: Known as *Smishing*, text messages disguised as trustworthy communication attempt to trick you into providing sensitive information or clicking malicious links. Smishing can make it easier for cybercriminals to access your private information and infiltrate your system.
    - **Other sources**: Many sites offer downloads that aren’t malicious, but may be combined with other software that is, or may just be unwanted. When installing software, always read any details and options in the installer to ensure you don’t accidentally agree to install additional, unwanted software. Like any other download, if you are unsure whether you would want the software, or are unfamiliar with what it does, research before agreeing to install.
