# Fireworks and dangerous materials

> [!NOTE]
> To ensure consistency worldwide, Microsoft Advertising enforces our policies across our ad network using the English version. We provide translated versions as a courtesy to our clients, though the translations are not meant to change the content or meaning of the official policy language.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This policy applies to all ad types.

> [!NOTE]
> This page was last updated on April 8, 2026.

Microsoft commits to safeguard individuals, communities, and properties from potential harm. It is our policy to not accept advertising campaigns that facilitate the sale of:

- **Fireworks:** This includes both consumer fireworks (e.g., sparklers, firecrackers) and display fireworks (e.g., aerial shells, large fireworks used in shows).
- **Explosives:** Specific types of explosives such as dynamite, TNT, blasting caps, and other similar materials.
- **Hazardous Materials:** This includes hyper-toxic chemicals, dangerous chemicals, and radioactive substances. Examples include mercury, lead, and asbestos.
- **Nuclear Materials:** Any materials related to nuclear energy or weapons.
- **Pyrotechnic Devices:** This includes flares, smoke bombs, and other devices designed to produce visible or audible effects by combustion.
- **Biological Hazards:** Pathogens, toxins, and other biological hazards that pose a threat to health and safety.
