# About audience signals

> [!NOTE]
> This feature is available for [Performance Max campaigns](hlp_BA_PROC_AboutPmax.md).

> [!NOTE]
> Not everyone has this feature yet. If you don't, don't worry—it's coming soon!

Audience signals help you find the right audience for your ads while maximizing conversions. When you create audience signals, you can choose the demographics and interests for your audience, which we'll use as a starting point to find relevant customers. You may also choose to find potential customers based on their LinkedIn profiles, or people who previously interacted with your website and products by adding [remarketing](hlp_BA_CONC_Audiences_Remarketing.md), [dynamic remarketing](hlp_BA_CONC_Audiences_ProductAudience.md), and [customer match lists](hlp_BA_CONC_Audiences_CustomerMatch.md).

> [!NOTE]
> Your ads may surface to audiences beyond those associated with your audience signal to find more customers and maximize conversions.

See [below](#view-your-audience-signal-status) for more information about viewing and understanding your audience signal status.

> [!NOTE]
> Not everyone can find potential customers people based on their LinkedIn profiles in Performance Max campaigns yet. If you can't, don't worry—it's coming soon!

## Add audience signals to your Performance Max campaigns

There are two ways to add audience signals to your campaign: [During campaign creation](#campaign-creation) and in your [asset group settings](#asset-group-settings).

> [!NOTE]
> You can also create audience signals at the account level in the [Audience library](#manage-your-audience-signals-in-the-audience-library), but you'll need to associate them with an asset group in your [asset group settings](#asset-group-settings).

<a id="campaign-creation"></a>

- **Campaign creation**

  1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Create**.
  1. Choose **Drive conversions** or **Sell products** as your campaign goal.
  1. Select **Performance Max**.
  1. Below **Political advertising declaration**, you need to declare whether your campaign is intended for political advertising. If your campaign isn't intended for political advertising, select the checkbox next to **By selecting Continue, I confirm that this campaign is not intended for political advertising**. Otherwise, you must remove any political advertising from your campaign and then proceed. [Learn more about how we define political advertising.](hlp_BA_CONC_Policy_RestrictedPolitical.md)
  1. Select **Continue**.
  1. Follow the steps provided by the campaign creation wizard to choose your campaign settings.
  1. Select **Save & go to next step**.
  1. Under **Audience signals** in your asset group settings, choose **Create** if you'd like to create a new audience signal or **Browse** if you want to add an existing audience signal.

     If you chose **Create**:

     1. Enter a **Name** for your audience signal.
     1. Select **Add interests** to find customers who have shown purchase intent signals within a particular category.
     1. Below **Your first-party data**, select **Add audience list** to find people who previously interacted with your website and products.
     1. Below **Demographics**, choose the **Age** and **Gender** of your potential customers. Then, choose the **Company**, **Industry**, and **Job function** of your potential customers based on their LinkedIn profiles.
     1. Select **Save**.

     If you chose **Browse**:

     1. Choose an existing audience signal.
     1. Select **Save**.
  1. Select **Save**.

  <a id="asset-group-settings"></a>

- **Asset group settings**

  1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Campaigns**.
  1. Select the appropriate Performance Max campaign.
  1. Select **Asset groups**.
  1. Select **Edit asset group**.
  1. Under **Audience signals**, choose **Create** if you'd like to create a new audience signal or **Browse** if you want to add an existing audience signal.

     If you chose **Create**:

     1. Enter a **Name** for your audience signal.
     1. Select **Add interests** to find customers who have shown purchase intent signals within a particular category.
     1. Below **Your first-party data**, select **Add audience list** to find people who previously interacted with your website and products.
     1. Below **Demographics**, choose the **Age** and **Gender** of your potential customers. Then, choose the **Company**, **Industry**, and **Job function** of your potential customers based on their LinkedIn profiles.
     1. Select **Save**.

     If you chose **Browse**:

     1. Choose an existing audience signal.
     1. Select **Save**.
  1. Select **Save**.

## Manage your audience signals in the Audience library

> [!NOTE]
> Not everyone has this feature yet. If you don't, don't worry—it's coming soon!

You can create, delete, and view the status for your audience signals at the account level in the Audience library:

- **Create an audience signal**

  1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Tools** > **Audiences**.
  1. Select **Audience signals**.
  1. Select **Create**.
  1. Enter a **Name** for your audience signal.
  1. Select **Add interests** to find customers who have shown purchase intent signals within a particular category.
  1. Below **Your first-party data**, select **Add audience list** to find people who previously interacted with your website and products.
  1. Below **Demographics**, choose the **Age** and **Gender** of your potential customers. Then, choose the **Company**, **Industry**, and **Job function** of your potential customers based on their LinkedIn profiles.
  1. Select **Save**.

  > [!NOTE]
  > After you create your audience signal, you'll need to associate it with an asset group in your [asset group settings](#asset-group-settings).

- **Delete an audience signal**

  1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Tools** > **Audiences**.
  1. Select **Audience signals**.
  1. Select the checkbox next to the appropriate audience signal.
  1. Select **Delete**.

  <a id="view-your-audience-signal-status"></a>

- **View your audience signal status**

  After you create your audience signal and associate it with an asset group in your [asset group settings](#asset-group-settings), you can view its status:

  1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Tools** > **Audiences**.
  1. Select **Audience signals**.
  1. Review the **Status** column.

  *Possible audience signal statuses*

  - **Ready:** No issues—audience signals are working fine.
  - **Building the audience:** It takes 24 hours for an audience to be built after you create a new audience signal or update an existing audience signal.
  - **Limited:** Audience may not serve due to issues with the associated audience signal. [Learn more about audience statuses](hlp_BA_CONC_Audiences_AudienceStatus.md).
