# About Ads Studio

> [!NOTE]
>
> - This feature is available in [all Microsoft Advertising markets](hlp_BA_CONC_Countries.md), except for generative AI features which aren't available in mainland China (AI image generation, brand kit, display templates, new image backgrounds, and video templates). It's available in [all languages that Microsoft Advertising supports](hlp_BA_CONC_AboutLanguageOptions.md).
> - If you upload assets from a Dropbox or Google Drive business account, your organization may restrict access and will need to unblock you. [Learn more](#about-restrictions-for-uploading-assets-from-non-microsoft-services)

Ads Studio is a creative performance tool that helps you create, find, manage, and optimize your assets so you can set up your ads for success. With Ads Studio you can curate the right assets to showcase in your ads for maximal performance—whether you're starting from scratch or enhancing existing media. Take advantage of AI-driven capabilities such as custom image generation, creating new image backgrounds, and our advanced content-based search to find assets faster. Additionally, Ads Studio offers predefined templates for you to build display and video assets with ease, and a brand kit that allows you to manage your brand identity across campaigns. These features are designed to save you time and effort as you curate the best assets to help meet your performance goals.

> [!NOTE]
> For more information about asset guidelines and accepted file types, see [Asset guidelines and accepted file types](hlp_BA_CONC_AssetGuidelines.md).

## Navigating the library

- **Organize your assets**

  - **Create a new folder**

    1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Ads Studio**.
    1. Select **Library**.
    1. Select **Add** > **Create folder**.
    1. Enter a name for your folder and save it.
    1. You can see how many subfolders and assets are in it from the folder thumbnails.

  - **Upload a folder from your computer**

    1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Ads Studio**.
    1. Select **Upload**.
    1. Select **Browse** to choose the folder and upload it to your Ads Studio library.

    > [!NOTE]
    > Images in subfolders will also be uploaded, and the parent-child structure will be preserved. You can see how many subfolders and assets are in it from the folder thumbnails.

  - **Move assets to a folder**

    1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Ads Studio**.
    1. Select **Library**.
    1. Select the appropriate asset(s).
    1. Select **Move to folder** in the blue action bar. You can also drag the asset into the folder.
    1. Select the folder.
    1. Select **Move here**.

- **Delete assets and folders**

  - **Delete an asset**

    1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Ads Studio**.
    1. Select **Library**.
    1. Search for your asset.
    1. Select the **Details** icon for the appropriate asset.
    1. Select **Delete**.

  - **Delete a folder**

    1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Ads Studio**.
    1. Select **Library**.
    1. Find the appropriate folder.
    1. Select the three vertical dots menu.
    1. Select **Delete**.

  - **Delete multiple assets or folders**

    1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Ads Studio**.
    1. Select **Library**.
    1. Select the appropriate assets or folders.
    1. Select **Delete** in the blue action bar.

    > [!NOTE]
    > You can't delete assets that ads, image extensions, or campaigns use unless you first remove their association. You also can't delete assets that you've associated with your account but haven't yet associated with ads or campaigns. See below for more information on how to remove these associations.

  - **Delete an asset that's associated with ads and campaigns**

    1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Ads Studio**.
    1. Select **Library**.
    1. Search for your asset using the asset ID or name.
    1. Select the **Details** icon for the appropriate asset.
    1. Select **View all ads using this image**.
    1. Select the pencil icon ![pencil icon](media/BA_icon_edit.png) next to the appropriate ad.
    1. Under **Image extensions**, remove the asset.
    1. Select **Save**.
    1. Follow the steps above to delete your asset.

  - **Delete an asset that's associated with image extensions**

    1. From the navigation menu on the left, hover over **Campaigns** and select **Ads & extensions** > **Extensions**.
    1. From the extensions dropdown, select **Image extensions**.
    1. Select the checkbox next to the appropriate image extension.
    1. Select **Edit** > **Delete association**.
    1. Follow the steps above to delete your asset.

  - **Delete an asset that's associated with your account**

    1. From the navigation menu on the left, hover over **Campaigns** and select **Ads & extensions** > **Extensions**.
    1. From the extensions dropdown, select **Image extensions**.
    1. Select **Create**.
    1. Under **Available image extensions**, remove your asset by selecting the “x”.
    1. Follow the steps above to delete your asset.

## About restrictions for uploading assets from non-Microsoft services

> [!NOTE]
> - Not everyone can upload assets from Dropbox or Google Drive yet. If you can't, don't worry—it's coming soon!
> - Uploading from OneDrive is not currently available, but will be soon.

If you upload assets from a Dropbox or Google Drive business account, your organization may restrict access and will need to unblock you. Here's where you can find more information about unblocking your access:

- **Google Drive**

  Some Google accounts may be unable to log in and upload due to administrator-enforced restrictions on app access. Google Workspace and Cloud Identity administrators can configure security policies that restrict or block other applications from using Google OAuth sign-in. If such restrictions are enabled, uploading will be blocked. This is controlled entirely by the organization's Admin Console security settings. For more information, see these pages:

  - [Control which third-party and internal apps access Google Workspace data](https://support.google.com/a/answer/13152743?hl=en)
  - [Control which apps access Google Workspace data](https://support.google.com/a/answer/7281227?hl=en)

- **Dropbox**

  For Dropbox Business and Enterprise accounts, team admins can block other apps. In such cases, individuals can't upload Dropbox files through Microsoft Advertising. This is controlled entirely by the admin and cannot be bypassed by the individual or Microsoft Advertising. For more information, see this page (under "Administrative actions"): [Control and Visibility with Dropbox](https://www.dropbox.com/business/trust/security/control-visibility).
