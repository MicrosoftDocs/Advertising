# About lift measurement

> [!NOTE]
> To access this feature, please reach out to your account manager.

Lift measurement helps you understand the impact your Audience campaigns (Display, Native, and Video) have on your advertising goals and objectives. This data allows you to fine-tune and enhance your Audience campaigns based on consumer activity.

Lift measurement includes an experiment group of consumers who are exposed to your ads and a control group of consumers who aren't exposed to them. We collect three measurement types that help you understand the full impact of your marketing funnel: Conversion lift, search lift, and visit lift. Conversion lift indicates how much more likely consumers are to make purchases after seeing your ads, whereas search lift and visit lift indicate how much more likely consumers are to actively search for your brand and visit your website after seeing your ads. See [below](#understanding-the-lift-measurement-types) for more information about the lift measurement types.

For example, say you're an advertiser for a writing assistant platform setting up a back-to-school campaign that targets students. You create a lift measurement experiment for your chosen Audience campaign and set it to run during the month of August so you can better understand whether potential customers are likely to take action after seeing your ads. This provides insight into the effectiveness of your campaign for your target audience.

<a id="understanding-the-lift-measurement-types"></a>

### Understanding the Lift measurement types

We report specific data points for each Lift measurement type:

Conversion lift

- **Conversion rate:** The number of people who converted on your website versus the total number of people in the group (experiment or control group).

Search lift

- **Brand search rate:** The number of people who made search queries that contain your brand, products, and/or services versus the number of people who made search queries containing keywords associated with your selected campaign(s).

Visit lift

- **Website visitation rate:** The number of people who visited your website versus the total number of people in the group (experiment or control group).

## Get started with Lift measurement

Follow these steps to create a Lift measurement experiment:

1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Tools** > **Measure results** > **Lift measurement**.
1. Select **Create**.
1. Choose the campaigns you want to include in the experiment.
1. Enter a **Name**.
1. Select your **Start date**.

   > [!NOTE]
   > Lift measurement runs for three weeks after the start date.
1. Choose the **Country** where you want to run the experiment. We'll measure results for ads delivered in the country/region you choose.
1. Below **Measurement types**, turn on the toggles for **Search lift** and/or **Visit and conversion lift**.

   - If you choose **Search lift**, you'll need to add keywords associated with *both* your campaign and brand name. You can also enter your customer ID to help measure results.

     > [!NOTE]
     > If you use different customer IDs for your Audience and Search campaigns, please ensure that you enter the customer ID associated with your Search campaigns.
   - If you choose **Visit and conversion lift**, you'll need to select **Measure visit and conversion lift**, **Measure conversion lift only**, or **Measure visit lift only**. Then, select the appropriate UET tag for your website so we can measure the results.
1. Select **Save**.

### Download your Lift measurement results

When your Lift measurement experiment is complete, you can download the results by following these steps:

1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Tools** > **Measure results** > **Lift measurement**.
1. In the table, select the link in the **Status** column for the appropriate Lift measurement experiment.
1. The CSV file will automatically download.
