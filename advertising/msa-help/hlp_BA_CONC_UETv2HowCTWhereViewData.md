# Where do I view conversion goal metric data?

> [!NOTE]
> In Microsoft Advertising, conversions are reported based on the time of the click that led to the conversion. Similarly, assists are reported based on the time of the click that contributed to a conversion.

There are three places in Microsoft Advertising where conversion data can be viewed:

- **Conversion goals page**

  : You can view the conversions, revenue, and repeat rate metrics. Select **Campaigns** at the top of the page, and then on the left pane, select **Conversion tracking** and then **Conversion goals**. Optionally, from the global menu at the top of the page, select **Tools** and then **Conversion goals**. You can also change the date range in the upper right corner of the page to control the time period for the metrics.

- **Campaigns**

  : The following conversion metrics can be added to the Campaigns, Ad groups, Ads, Keywords, and Ad extensions tables by selecting **Columns** and then **Modify columns**.

  *Conversion rate and CPA are calculated based on clicks that could lead to conversions (e.g., clicks after a conversion goal was enabled). Conversion rate = (Conversions ÷ Eligible interactions) × 100. CPA (cost per acquisition) = Total cost ÷ Conversions. Eligible interactions include clicks and other qualifying interactions that occurred during the conversion window and after a conversion goal was enabled. Interactions excluded by attribution settings, conversion windows, or account filters are not counted in these calculations.*

  - Conversions
  - Conversion rate
  - Revenue
  - CPA
  - Revenue on ad spend

- **Reports**: The following reports include conversion data.


  - **Conversions report**

    Conversion related metrics you will see are Conversions, Conversion rate, CPA, Assists, Cost per assist, Revenue, Revenue per conversion, and Revenue per assist.

    > [!NOTE]
    > - Conversions and goals are only reported for campaigns and ad groups with keywords.
    > - For Shopping campaign conversions and goals you can use the product partition, product unit partition, product search term, and product dimension reports.
    > - For Dynamic Search ads (DSA) conversions and goals you can use the DSA auto target, DSA category, and DSA search term reports.

  - **Conversions model comparison**

    Conversion model comparison provides reporting for advertisers to compare the performance of attribution models side-by-side. This helps in finding keywords, ad group, and campaigns that are undervalued when using the last interaction attribution model (LIA) and the data driven attribution model (DDA).

  - **Goals report**

    > [!NOTE]
    > - Conversions and goals are only reported for campaigns and ad groups with keywords.
    > - For Shopping campaign conversions and goals you can use the product partition, product unit partition, product search term, and product dimension reports.
    > - For Dynamic Search ads (DSA) conversions and goals you can use the DSA auto target, DSA category, and DSA search term reports.

  - **Ad Dynamic Text and Share of Voice reports**
    Conversion related metrics you will see are Conversions, Conversion rate, CPA.

  - **Most other reports**

    - Account
    - Campaign
    - Ad Groups
    - Ads
    - Keywords
    - Search Terms
    - Destination URL
    - Website URL (publisher)
    - Ad Extension by Keyword
    - Ad Extension by Ad
    - Geo location (old and new versions)
    - Product Partition
    - Product Unit Partition
    - Product Dimension
