# Beauty and cosmetics

> [!NOTE]
> To ensure consistency worldwide, Microsoft Advertising enforces our policies across our ad network using the English version. We provide translated versions as a courtesy to our clients, though the translations are not meant to change the content or meaning of the official policy language.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This policy applies to all ad types.

> [!NOTE]
> This page was last updated on April 22, 2026.

This policy outlines the principles that guide responsible and truthful promotion of beauty, skincare, and personal care products across all media channels. It is designed to ensure that marketing practices reflect industry standards, protect consumer trust, and comply with applicable regulations.

> [!NOTE]
> Our advertising policies for healthcare and pharmaceutical products differ significantly due to their clinical nature and regulatory oversight. For information specific to healthcare and pharmaceutical advertising campaigns, please visit [Healthcare and Pharmaceutical Ad Policy](hlp_BA_CONC_Policy_RestrictedPharma.md).

To ensure consumer safety and maintain advertising integrity, all ads promoting cosmetics and beauty products must comply with the following policies:

- **General Requirements**

  - Products advertised must be safe for consumer use and must not be subject to recalls or bans by recognized health authorities (e.g., FDA, EMA, MHRA, TGA).
  - Prescription-only products must not be advertised as cosmetics. For advertising standards related to prescription or therapeutic products, please refer to [Healthcare and Pharmaceutical Ad Policy](hlp_BA_CONC_Policy_RestrictedPharma.md).
  - Claims such as “FDA-approved” may only be used if the product has received official approval from the FDA. Equivalent claims must be substantiated by the appropriate authority in the target market.
  - Use of official logos (e.g., FDA, WHO) or misleading health-related claims is strictly prohibited.
  - Ads must not claim to treat, cure, or prevent diseases unless the product is officially approved as a drug or therapeutic agent by the relevant regulatory body.

- **Ad Content Restrictions**

  - Ads must not be misleading or promote false, unrealistic, or extreme results.
  - Any "before and after" comparisons must be truthful and not presented in a deceptive or exaggerated manner.
  - Images used in ads must accurately represent the product's capabilities. Manipulation of visuals beyond what the cosmetic product alone can achieve is not permitted.
