# Understanding egregious violation suspensions

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This page was last updated on June 27, 2025.

Microsoft Advertising is committed to a safe online environment. Egregious violations of our terms or policies warrant the immediate suspension of the advertiser's account. We consider egregious those violations that involve illegal content or activity, pose a threat to user safety or security, or degrade or impair the security of the services provided by Microsoft or its partners. These violations often indicate intent to use our service in a fraudulent, harmful, or illegal manner. If we detect an egregious violation, we will suspend the advertiser's access to the service, including access to all information contained therein, immediately and without prior warning.

- **Examples of Egregious Violations Include, But Are Not Limited To:**

  - Obfuscation of advertiser or payer identity, including providing inaccurate identity information or documentation.
  - Use of the service to facilitate or promote illegal practices, content, products and services.
  - Malicious software codes (e.g., malware, phishing, scareware, ransomware, pirated software).
  - Counterfeit, malicious, or disingenuous content or services, or any other content which poses a threat to the security of our users or our service.
  - Any landing page or downloadable content that removes user control.
  - Ad content which obfuscates the intention or functionality of any downloaded product or provided service.
  - Circumventing or evading system service safeguards or monitoring.
  - Any content or action designed to facilitate gaining unauthorized access to any user, advertiser, or data, or to manipulate the Microsoft Advertising service.
  - Deceptive or obfuscated content, cloaking, or spoofing.
  - Impersonation, manipulation or deceptive media, or deceptive use of name or brand to imply endorsement.
  - Unauthorized collection of personal information.
  - Emotionally manipulative or coercive content.
  - Offensive, obscene, defamatory, or threatening content, or any content intended to degrade, intimidate or incite violence.
  - Attempting to circumvent our account suspension or other measures intended to enforce our policies or terms by creating a new advertiser account or Manager Account.

- **What Happens After an Immediate Suspension?**

  We will notify the advertiser via email of the reason for the suspension with information on how to appeal the decision or address the concern. The advertiser will not be able to use Microsoft Advertising services until they redress the violation to our satisfaction.

- **Are Immediate Suspensions Appealable?**

  Advertisers may appeal an immediate suspension by [Contacting Support](https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=398371) within six months of the disapproval or suspension decision. If the advertiser redresses the violation, we will lift all applicable penalties from their account.

  Once an appeal is submitted, reviews typically take seven to 10 business days. Super Admins will be notified of the resolution via email. If you have not received a response after 10 business days, contact Microsoft Advertising [Support](https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=398371) to check on the appeal status.
