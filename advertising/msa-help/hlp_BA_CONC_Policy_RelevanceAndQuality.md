# Ad relevance and landing page quality

> [!NOTE]
> To ensure consistency worldwide, Microsoft Advertising enforces our policies across our ad network using the English version. We provide translated versions as a courtesy to our clients, though the translations are not meant to change the content or meaning of the official policy language.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This policy applies to all ad types.

> [!NOTE]
> This page was last updated on April 8, 2026.

To provide users with a quality experience, we require that your ads closely align with the products, services, or information featured on your landing page, as well as the general content, purpose, and theme of your site.

## Related policies:

- [General Ad Requirements](hlp_BA_CONC_Policy_GeneralAdRequirements.md)
- [Information Integrity and Misleading Content](hlp_BA_CONC_Policy_DisallowedInformation.md)

## Landing page quality

Landing pages should help users quickly find the products, services, or information relevant to the ad. Landing pages should provide clear, direct access to content that is related to your ads and keywords. Landing page and site content should exist to provide value, not solely or primarily to display ads or attract traffic.

Sites or landing pages are prohibited if they:

- Use doorway pages or cloaking.
- Redirect site visitors unexpectedly to unrelated domains.
- Make changes without the user's consent, including:

  - Changing browser preferences or resetting default home pages.
  - Resizing browser windows or disabling browser buttons.
- Do not open properly or consistently result in a “product not available” message.
- Display a high density of advertising above the fold, and/or consist significantly of advertising or links.<br>

  :::image type="content" source="media/Policies_CreativeSpecs3.png" alt-text="Do not display a high density of advertising above the fold.":::
- Feature content that functions primarily to support ad monetization.
- Are built for search engines and feature excessive off-topic keywords or hard-to-read text.
- Consist entirely of advertising or whose primary purpose is to direct users to advertising.
- Feature slideshow or gallery content that requires the user to click more than 5 times in the slideshow to get to the content mentioned in the ad.
- Contain sparse or limited content, particularly where the user would expect to find a range of offers, products, or information on a similar site.<br>

  :::image type="content" source="media/Policies_CreativeSpecs4.png" alt-text="Do not feature sparse or limited content.":::
- Require the entry of sensitive personal data unnecessary to provide the service or complete the purchase.<br>

  :::image type="content" source="media/Policies_CreativeSpecs5.png" alt-text="Do not require the entry of unnecessary personal data.":::
- Delay or obstruct the user's access to requested content, products, or services by adding steps solely designed to monetize the user.<br>

  :::image type="content" source="media/Policies_CreativeSpecs6.png" alt-text="Do not delay or obstruct the user's access to requested content, products, or services.":::
- Employ marketing tactics that are evasive, overly sensational, or potentially confusing.
- Reference or link to fake news reports containing unsubstantiated, unsourced claims.
- Are link farms.
- Are parked domains.

Advertorial landing pages must be clearly labeled as an Advertisement or Advertorial. The labeling must be at the top of the page, in the same font size as the main font on the advertorial or larger, and in a font color that stands out from the page background.

- Ads that appear as editorial and use labels or language such as Breaking News, Shocking Discovery, Special Report, Consumer News, Consumer Alerts, etc., that may make a user believe they are viewing editorial/news content are prohibited.

## Landing page functionality

The functionality of your site should not surprise site visitors or interfere with a user's ability to navigate; disruptive or deceptive content is not allowed. Navigation buttons should operate in a standard and predictable manner, and all parts of the page should load properly.

- Your landing page must be available and reliably responsive even at peak use times.
- Your final URL must resolve to a single static landing page; it may not take users to a different page on the site each time the ad is clicked.
- All URLs within an ad group must use the same base domain. Ads or keywords that reference different domains within the same ad group are not permitted. (Example: the base domain for ads.microsoft.com/myURL is microsoft.com)
- You must use a landing page that you own or control, or that you are authorized to represent or send traffic to.
- The landing page must not:

  - Generate any disruptive pop-ups or pop-unders.
      > [!NOTE]
      > Functionalities that are part of the site's expected experience or otherwise non-intrusive or user initiated, such as an age verification log-in or cookie verification, are acceptable.
  - Spawn multiple pop-ups or any pop-up that prevents visitors from leaving the site.<br>

    :::image type="content" source="media/Policies_CreativeSpecs1.png" alt-text="Do not spawn multiple pop-ups or any pop-up that prevents visitors from leaving the site.":::
  - Open any additional windows.
  - Mislead the user with "fake" dialog boxes or browser windows.
  - Be designed to look broken or simulate a non-working webpage in any way.
  - Sites driving users directly to a sign-up or login page must link back to the main home page or supporting content describing services and terms of use.

Users must be able to easily access the terms of service or terms and conditions before they create a sign-up or login.

- It must be noted in ad copy if access to content or services requires a software download (e.g., toolbars):<br>

  :::image type="content" source="media/Policies_CreativeSpecs7.png" alt-text="Note in ad copy if access to content or services requires a software download.":::
- Landing pages must not use "fake" close behavior. For example, when a user clicks a "close" button on the page, it should close that page, and no other behavior should result:<br>

  :::image type="content" source="media/Policies_CreativeSpecs2.png" alt-text="Do not use &quot;fake&quot; close behavior.":::
