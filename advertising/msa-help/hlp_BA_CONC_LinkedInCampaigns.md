# About LinkedIn campaigns

> [!NOTE]
> Not everyone has this feature yet. If you don't, don't worry—it's coming soon!

Create and manage Sponsored Content ads that engage your company's LinkedIn audience. You can serve ads with text and images that target LinkedIn users by location, profile language, company, industry, job function, job title, and job seniority.

Before creating your campaign, you must first [request permission](#request-permission) to serve Sponsored Content ads as a [paid media admin](https://www.linkedin.com/help/linkedin/answer/a554540) for your company's LinkedIn page. Your request will be sent to the administrator of the company's page for approval.

## Create a LinkedIn campaign

If you've already been granted permission to serve ads from the administrator of the company's LinkedIn page, see [below](#create-your-campaign). To request permission, follow these steps:

<a id="request-permission"></a>

- **Request permission**

  1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Create**.
  1. Choose **Build brand awareness** or **Generate leads** as your campaign goal.
  1. Select **LinkedIn**.
  1. Select **Continue**.
  1. Select **Connect**.
  1. When prompted, sign in to your LinkedIn account with your email or phone number and password.
  1. Below **Connect to your company's page**, search for your company.

     > [!NOTE]
     > Once you connect to your company's LinkedIn page, you won't be able to connect your Microsoft Advertising account to a different company.
  1. Select the checkbox next to the terms of agreement, acknowledging that the [LinkedIn Ads Agreement](https://www.linkedin.com/legal/sas-terms) will govern your LinkedIn campaigns.
  1. Select **Next**.
  1. Select **Continue** to acknowledge that once you connect to your company's LinkedIn page, you won't be able to connect your Microsoft Advertising account to a different company.
  1. Select **Request permission**. Your request will automatically be sent to the company's page administrator for approval.

  Once the company's page administrator approves your request, you can successfully create your campaign.

- **Permissions approval on LinkedIn**

  The company's page administrator will either approve or deny your request under **Paid media admins** > **Sponsored content poster** on LinkedIn.

  <a id="create-your-campaign"></a>

- **Create your campaign**

  1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Create**.
  1. Choose **Build brand awareness** or **Generate leads** as your campaign goal.
  1. Select **LinkedIn**.
  1. Select **Continue**.
  1. Enter your **Campaign name**.
  1. Enter the **Final URL**.
  1. Enter the **Introductory text**.

     > [!NOTE]
     > This is the text that appears at the top of your LinkedIn ad. It should be under 150 characters to avoid truncation. If the text is over 150 characters, it may be truncated.
  1. Add the image you want to appear in your ad.

     > [!NOTE]
     > Use the ad preview area on the right to see how your ads would look like in different placements. You can also edit your image by selecting **Edit aspect ratio**.
  1. Enter the **Headline**.
  1. Select the **Call to action**.
  1. Select **Save & go to next step**.
  1. Select the **Location** to reach LinkedIn users based on where they live.
  1. Choose the **Language** based on the profile language of your target audience.

     > [!NOTE]
     > You should write your ad copy in the language that you choose. Once you begin publishing ads, you cannot change the language. If you choose English, your campaign will target all LinkedIn member accounts in your selected **Location**, regardless of their profile language settings. If you choose a language other than English, your ads will appear to member accounts that meet your target audience criteria and match your language.
  1. Choose your audience targeting for **Company**, **Industry**, **Job function**, **Job title**, **Job seniority**, **Gender**, and **Age**.
  1. Choose the conversion goals that you want to add to your campaign. Conversion goals measure the actions that people take on your website after clicking or viewing your ad on LinkedIn.

     > [!NOTE]
     > If you haven't created a conversion goal that's compatible with LinkedIn, you can do so in **Tools** > **Conversions** > **Conversion goals**. Then, after creating your campaign, go to your campaign settings and select the appropriate conversion goal below **Conversions**.
  1. Select **Save & go to next step**.
  1. Enter the **Budget**.
  1. Choose your **Bid strategy**. To learn more about the available bid strategies, see below.
  1. Select the **Start date** and **End date** for your campaign.
  1. Select **Save & go to next step**.
  1. Review the Summary page to ensure that the information is correct.
  1. When you're finished, select **Go live** or **Save & exit**.

## About bid strategies for LinkedIn campaigns

If you chose **Build brand awareness** as your campaign goal, then the available bid strategies are Maximize reach and Maximize impressions. If you chose **Generate leads** as your campaign goal, then the available bid strategy is Maximize clicks.

Each bid strategy leverages LinkedIn's [Maximum delivery](https://www.linkedin.com/help/lms/answer/a423490?hcppcid=search) bidding strategy with the following:

- Maximize reach optimizes your budget toward delivering ads to the maximum number of unique member accounts possible.
- Maximize impressions optimizes your budget toward delivering the maximum number of ad impressions possible.
- Maximize clicks optimizes your budget toward delivering the maximum number of ad clicks possible.
