# About China advertiser identity verification

Microsoft Advertising requires advertiser identity verification to ensure that customers see ads from trusted sources to improve their overall experience. In China, we request advertisers to become a verified advertiser before they can deliver their ads.

When we require advertiser identity verification for your account, we'll send you an email and an in-product notification with steps to complete your verification. We'll use the documentation you provide to verify your business name and location.

## Understanding the advertiser identity verification process

All advertisers are required to complete advertiser identity verification (AIV) and other applicable required qualification(s) before their ads can serve. Anytime Microsoft Advertising requires advertiser identity verification, you'll receive an email request and an in-product notification for one or more of your accounts listing verification instructions.

Advertisers may also need to add additional qualifications, such as those for celebrity endorsements, industry, and patents.

> [!NOTE]
> Our review process may take one to three business days. If your verification fails, your account(s) will be paused. If that happens, you can reapply for verification by following the steps below.

## Get started

In China, you can only choose to verify as a business.

1. From the navigation menu on the left, hover over **Settings** and select **Account verifications**.

   > [!NOTE]
   > If you have multiple accounts, choose one from the account dropdown menu on the top left.
1. Under **Advertiser identify verification (AIV) status**, select **Become a verified advertiser**.
1. Select **Verify as a business**.
1. Review and verify your account's business name and location.

   > [!NOTE]
   > The information here must match that in the documents you submit for verification later, or else your verification will fail. You can make updates in your account settings to make sure that your information is up to date.
1. Enter information about your legally registered business, including **Business website** and **Business email address**.
1. Upon entering the **Business email address**, request a verification code by selecting **Send verification code**. You'll receive an email at your business email address with the verification code.
1. Enter the verification code from the email under **Enter code**.

   > [!NOTE]
   > If you don't receive a verification code, you may request additional verification codes once **Send Verification** is re-enabled.
1. Under **Unified Social Credit Identifier**, please provide the identifier on your business license. .

   > [!NOTE]
   > It should be an 18-character length identifier, containing numbers (and capital letters)
1. Under **Business license**, provide documentation with your business name and address that'll be used for verification.
1. If you own registered rights to your domain, under **Website documents**, submit documentation that proves your business has registered rights to the domain (e.g., receipt of domain purchase or domain renewal).

   > [!NOTE]
   > If you provided an email address besides your business domain, then you also need to provide documentation to verify your personal identity.
1. Select **Next**.
1. Review the information.
1. Select **Submit application**.
