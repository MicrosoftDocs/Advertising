# Singapore Goods and Services Tax

In Singapore, billing is managed by Microsoft Ireland Operations Ltd., in Ireland.

Effective January 1, 2024, Microsoft Advertising is required to charge an 9% Singapore Goods and Services Tax rate (GST). Accounts without a valid GST registration number will be charged 9% GST on billing documents issued on or after January 1, 2024.If you provide your GST registration number, Microsoft Advertising will apply GST at 0%.

> [!NOTE]
> The tax rate is subject to change.

Microsoft Advertising cannot provide guidance on tax or VAT. Please contact your tax advisor or local tax office if you have questions.

## How to change your tax identification number

1. From the navigation menu on the left, hover over **Settings** and select **Account settings**.
1. Next to **Account information**, select ![Edit account settings](media/BA_icon_edit.png) **Edit account settings**.
1. Enter your **GST registration number**, located below your **Business address**.
1. Select **Save**.

If you want to bulk update multiple accounts that use the same GST registration number, please follow these steps:

1. From the navigation menu on the left, select **Tools** > **All accounts** > **Accounts summary**.
1. Select the **Management** tab.
1. Select the accounts for which you want to upload the **GST registration number**.
1. Select **Edit** > **Business location**.
1. Enter your **GST registration number**, located below your **Business address**.
1. Select **Save**.

## How Microsoft Advertising will apply taxes

- **For postpay threshold or monthly invoice accounts:** The 9% GST will be added as a percentage of your net charges. For example, if you've generated S$100 SGD in advertising charges, you'd be assessed S$9 SGD for taxes.
- **For prepay accounts:** Some of your prepay funds will go towards taxes. For example, if you added S$100 SGD to your prepay account, S$8.26 SGD would cover taxes, and the remaining S$91.74 SGD would cover advertising charges.
